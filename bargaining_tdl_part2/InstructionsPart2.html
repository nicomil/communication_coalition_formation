In Part 2, we will ask you to express your preference between bets on given probabilities and bets on the choice made in Part 1 by the other participants of your group.

In Part 1 you are matched with the player on the left and the player on the right, we will present to you six questions for each player; the first six questions will refer to one player and the last six to the remaining one. In each question, you will be presented with two options as in the following example:

  • Option 1 (other player choice option): You win $ 5 if the player on the left(right) chooses “I would like to divide the $ 12 equally with you only” or “I would like to divide the $ 12 equally among all the members of the group”, and you win nothing otherwise.

  • Option 2 (given probability option): You win $ 5 with various probability values (p%) and nothing otherwise.

In all questions, as in the above example, you will be offered the chance to win $ 5. You will be asked to state which one of these two options you prefer for p%, from 0 to 100. Let us illustrate it with the example given above for some values of p:

  • Imagine that the random value of the given probability option (p) selected at the end of the experiment is equal to p = 100, you will most likely prefer Option 2 because you are then guaranteed to win $ 5.

  • Imagine that the random value of random value of the given probability option (p) is equal to p = 0, then you will most likely prefer Option 1, as you might win something, as opposed to Option 2, where there is no chance of you to win at all.
